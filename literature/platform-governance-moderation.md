# Literature Review: Platform Governance, Content Moderation, and Prosocial Design

**Paper:** Protocol Love: Constitutive Protocol Design for Prosocial Worlding  
**Author:** Botao Amber Hu  
**Subdomain:** Platform Governance, Content Moderation, Nudge Theory, Community Norms, Prosocial Design, Behavioral Design

---

## Foundational Theories

### 1. Lessig, L. (1999). *Code and Other Laws of Cyberspace*. Basic Books.
- **URL:** https://lessig.org/images/resources/1999-Code.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Foundational argument that "code is law" — the architecture of cyberspace (software, hardware, protocols) functions as a regulatory force alongside law, markets, and social norms. Code sets permissions, creates affordances, and blocks uses; those who control the code control behavior. Four modalities of regulation: law, norms, markets, and architecture.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** The most direct theoretical ancestor of Protocol Love's thesis. Lessig establishes that technical architecture constitutively shapes behavior — Protocol Love extends this from cyberspace generally to game and platform protocols specifically, arguing that prosociality can be designed at the architectural/protocol level.

### 2. Lessig, L. (2006). *Code: Version 2.0*. Basic Books.
- **URL:** https://cyberlaw.stanford.edu/publications/code-and-other-laws-cyberspace-version-20/
- **Key contribution:** Updated edition reflecting the evolution of internet regulation. Argues that under commercial pressure, cyberspace is becoming more regulated (not less), and that citizens must choose what values code embodies. Emphasizes that the lack of ownership (commons) is key to checking governmental and corporate control.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Reinforces that protocol design is a political and ethical act — choices about code/architecture embed values. Protocol Love argues these choices should constitutively embed prosociality.

### 3. Thaler, R. H., & Sunstein, C. R. (2008). *Nudge: Improving Decisions about Health, Wealth, and Happiness*. Yale University Press.
- **ISBN:** 978-0-300-12223-7
- **Key contribution:** Introduces nudge theory and choice architecture — the idea that how choices are presented (defaults, feedback, structuring options) predictably alters behavior without restricting freedom. Defines libertarian paternalism: designing environments that steer people toward better choices while preserving autonomy. Tools: defaults, expecting error, understanding mappings, giving feedback, structuring complex choices, creating incentives.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Nudge theory provides a behavioral science foundation for Protocol Love's claim that designed environments (protocols) shape behavior. However, Protocol Love goes beyond nudges (which adjust choice presentation) to argue for *constitutive* design — protocols that make prosociality the emergent, natural outcome of participation.

### 4. Thaler, R. H., Sunstein, C. R., & Balz, J. P. (2012). Choice Architecture. In E. Shafir (Ed.), *The Behavioral Foundations of Public Policy* (Ch. 25). Princeton University Press.
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1583509
- **Key contribution:** Formalizes choice architecture as a design discipline. Analyzes tools available to choice architects: defaults, expecting error, understanding mappings, giving feedback, structuring complex choices, and creating incentives. Shows how choice architecture can be used to nudge people toward better decisions.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Provides the design vocabulary for understanding how protocol-level decisions (defaults, feedback loops, incentive structures) constitute choice architectures that shape prosocial or antisocial outcomes.

### 5. Kraut, R. E., & Resnick, P. (2012). *Building Successful Online Communities: Evidence-Based Social Design*. MIT Press.
- **ISBN:** 978-0-262-52891-7
- **Key contribution:** Translates social science theory (social psychology, economics) into actionable design claims for online communities. Covers encouraging contributions (public goods theory), building commitment (interpersonal bond formation), regulating behavior (norm enforcement), and dealing with newcomers (socialization). Each design claim is supported by empirical evidence.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** The closest precursor to Protocol Love in its systematic approach to designing prosocial outcomes. Kraut & Resnick treat community design as applied social science; Protocol Love extends this by arguing that the protocol layer itself — not just features layered on top — should constitutively produce prosociality.

## Moderation Taxonomies and Frameworks

### 6. Grimmelmann, J. (2015). The Virtues of Moderation. *Yale Journal of Law & Technology*, 17, 42–109.
- **URL:** https://james.grimmelmann.net/files/articles/virtues-of-moderation.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Definitive taxonomy of online community moderation. Breaks moderation into four "verbs" (exclusion, pricing, organizing, norm-setting) and multiple "adverbs" (automatically/manually, transparently/secretly, ex ante/ex post). Case studies of Wikipedia, MetaFilter, and Reddit. Shows how moderation helps communities navigate between chaos (too much freedom) and sterility (too much control).
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Grimmelmann's taxonomy describes moderation as governance overlay; Protocol Love argues for pushing prosociality deeper — into the constitutive protocols themselves rather than relying on after-the-fact moderation. The "organizing" and "norm-setting" verbs are closest to Protocol Love's constitutive approach.

### 7. Seering, J. (2020). Reconsidering Community Self-Moderation: the Role of Research in Supporting Community-Based Models for Online Content Moderation. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 4(CSCW2), Article 107. DOI: 10.1145/3415178
- **URL:** https://joseph.seering.org/papers/Seering_2020_Reconsidering_Community_Self_Moderation.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Comprehensive review of community self-moderation research. Argues for moving beyond reactive, platform-driven moderation toward community-based models. Synthesizes structural/functional perspectives (Grimmelmann), user perspectives, and moderator perspectives. Calls for research that supports rather than supplants community judgment.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Seering's call to move "beyond content removal" toward community-based, proactive moderation aligns with Protocol Love's argument for constitutive design. Both advocate for governance embedded in community structure rather than imposed from outside.

### 8. Gillespie, T. (2018). *Custodians of the Internet: Platforms, Content Moderation, and the Hidden Decisions That Shape Social Media*. Yale University Press.
- **ISBN:** 978-0-300-17313-0
- **Key contribution:** Examines how platforms moderate content and the hidden decisions, values, and labor involved. Argues that moderation is not peripheral to platforms but central to their function. Platforms are not neutral conduits but active shapers of public discourse through moderation policies and technical affordances.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Establishes that platform design is never neutral — moderation decisions are constitutive of the social environment. Protocol Love extends this insight by arguing that if moderation is always constitutive, it should be constitutively *prosocial* by design.

## Norms, Rules, and Community Governance

### 9. Chandrasekharan, E., Samory, M., Jhaver, S., Charvat, H., Bruckman, A., Lampe, C., Eisenstein, J., & Gilbert, E. (2018). The Internet's Hidden Rules: An Empirical Study of Reddit Norm Violations at Micro, Meso, and Macro Scales. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 2(CSCW), Article 32. DOI: 10.1145/3274301
- **URL:** https://courses.cs.umbc.edu/graduate/691/spring23/pdf/3274301.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Large-scale empirical study of Reddit norms at three scales: macro (site-wide, e.g., no personal attacks), meso (shared across many communities), and micro (community-specific). Demonstrates that norms are emergent, context-dependent, and multi-layered. Proposes that macro/meso/micro norm framework can provide sensible defaults for new communities.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that norms operate at multiple scales and are constituted by community interaction. Protocol Love argues that protocols can embed norms at the deepest (macro) level, making prosociality the default rather than requiring enforcement.

### 10. Fiesler, C., Jiang, J. A., McCann, J., Frye, K., & Brubaker, J. R. (2018). Reddit Rules! Characterizing an Ecosystem of Governance. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*, 12(1).
- **URL:** https://cmci.colorado.edu/~cafi5706/icwsm18-redditrules.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Mixed-methods study of 100,000 subreddits and their rules. Characterizes rule types, prevalence, and how they vary with community size and type. Shows rules are context-dependent but share common features across the site. Motivates inquiry into mechanisms of rule formation and enforcement.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows that rule-making is a core governance activity in online communities. Protocol Love argues for moving governance from explicit rules (which require enforcement) to constitutive protocols (which produce prosociality through design).

### 11. Leibmann, L., Weld, G., Zhang, A. X., & Althoff, T. (2025). Reddit Rules and Rulers: Quantifying the Link Between Rules and Perceptions of Governance Across Thousands of Communities. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14163
- **Key contribution:** Largest-to-date analysis of Reddit rules (67,545 rules across 5,225 communities). Classifies rules using 17-attribute taxonomy. Finds that rules addressing participation, formatting, and commercial activities are most strongly associated with positive governance perceptions. Adding rules immediately improves perceptions, but the effect diminishes after six months.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that explicit rules have diminishing returns — their effect decays. Protocol Love's constitutive approach argues for governance mechanisms that don't decay because prosociality is built into the interaction structure itself.

### 12. Matias, J. N. (2019). Preventing Harassment and Increasing Group Participation Through Social Norms in 2,190 Online Science Discussions. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 116(20), 9785–9789. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1813486116
- **Key contribution:** Large-scale field experiment on r/science showing that posting community rules as "sticky comments" significantly reduces rule-breaking behavior and increases newcomer participation. Demonstrates that norm reminders — a simple design intervention — can shift behavior at scale.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Provides empirical evidence that design interventions shape prosocial behavior. However, Matias's intervention is a *reminder* layered on top of existing protocols; Protocol Love argues for embedding prosociality more deeply, at the constitutive protocol level.

### 13. Weld, G., Leibmann, L., Zhang, A. X., & Althoff, T. (2024). Perceptions of Moderators as a Large-Scale Measure of Online Community Governance. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.16610
- **Key contribution:** Assesses governance on Reddit by classifying sentiment of community members' public discussion of their moderators (1.89 million posts). Finds that moderators who are active community members are perceived more favorably, and that strict enforcement is valued differently across community types.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows that governance perception depends on community context and moderator embeddedness. Supports Protocol Love's argument that governance should be integrated into community practice (constitutive) rather than imposed from above.

## Transparency and Moderation Effects

### 14. Jhaver, S., Bruckman, A., & Gilbert, E. (2019). Does Transparency in Moderation Really Matter? User Behavior After Content Removal Explanations on Reddit. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 3(CSCW), Article 150. DOI: 10.1145/3359252
- **URL:** https://shagunjhaver.com/research/articles/jhaver-2019-transparency/jhaver-2019-transparency.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Analysis of 32 million Reddit posts showing that removal explanations educate users about community norms and reduce odds of future post removals. Explanations from bots were as effective as those from human moderators. Proposes that transparency in moderation helps users learn social norms.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that feedback mechanisms shape norm internalization. Protocol Love argues for protocols that make norms self-evident through interaction design rather than requiring post-hoc explanation.

### 15. Jhaver, S., Appling, D. S., Gilbert, E., & Bruckman, A. (2019). "Did You Suspect the Post Would Be Removed?": Understanding User Reactions to Content Removals on Reddit. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 3(CSCW), Article 192. DOI: 10.1145/3359294
- **Key contribution:** Qualitative study of user reactions to content removal on Reddit. Many users did not understand why their content was removed. Highlights the gap between moderator intent and user understanding, and the emotional toll of opaque moderation.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Illustrates the limitations of reactive moderation — users experience removal as arbitrary. Protocol Love's constitutive approach aims to prevent the need for removal by designing protocols where prosocial behavior is the natural outcome.

### 16. Srinivasan, K. B., Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil, C., Lee, L., & Tan, C. (2019). Content Removal as a Moderation Strategy: Compliance and Other Outcomes in the ChangeMyView Community. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 3(CSCW), Article 163. DOI: 10.1145/3359265
- **Key contribution:** Studies content removal on r/ChangeMyView. Finds that removal reduces recidivism — removed users are less likely to have future posts removed. However, removal also reduces overall participation.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows the trade-off of reactive moderation: it reduces bad behavior but also reduces participation. Protocol Love argues for constitutive design that avoids this trade-off by making prosociality the default mode of engagement.

## Proactive and Prosocial Design Interventions

### 17. Chang, J. P., Schluger, C., & Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil, C. (2022). Thread With Caution: Proactively Helping Users Assess and Deescalate Tension in Their Online Discussions. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 6(CSCW2), Article 398. DOI: 10.1145/3555101
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.01401
- **Key contribution:** Proposes a "risk awareness" paradigm for proactive moderation — empowering well-intentioned users with information about conversation derailment risk. Develops and deploys a tool on Wikipedia Talk Pages. Finds that warned users tend to de-escalate, while unwarned users tend to escalate tension.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates proactive, user-empowering moderation that works by shaping awareness rather than removing content. Aligns with Protocol Love's vision of constitutive design that shapes behavior through environmental signals rather than punishment.

### 18. Schluger, C., Chang, J. P., & Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil, C. (2022). Proactive Moderation of Online Discussions: Existing Practices and the Potential for Algorithmic Support. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 6(CSCW2). DOI: 10.1145/3555116
- **URL:** https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~cristian/Proactive_Moderation_files/proactive_moderation.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Studies proactive moderation on Wikipedia Talk Pages through mixed methods. Finds moderators already engage in proactive practices despite lacking tool support. Proposes a framework for algorithmic assistance in proactive moderation, moving beyond reactive content removal.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates the shift from reactive to proactive moderation paradigms. Protocol Love pushes further — from proactive intervention to constitutive design where the protocol structure itself prevents the conditions that require intervention.

### 19. Horta Ribeiro, M., et al. (2025). Guidance for Online Communities. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 9(CSCW).
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2411.16814
- **Key contribution:** Introduces "post guidance" — a proactive moderation approach that guides users' contributions using rules that trigger interventions as users draft content. Evaluated on Reddit with 97,616 users across 33 subreddits. Increases successful posts, decreases moderator workload, increases contribution quality, with no impact on user activity.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Post guidance represents a step toward constitutive design — intervening at the moment of creation rather than after the fact. Protocol Love argues for going further: designing protocols where the interaction structure itself constitutively shapes prosocial contributions.

### 20. Grüning, D., & Kamin, J. (2025). Prosocial Design in Trust and Safety. In *Trust and Safety Handbook*. Prosocial Design Network.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.12792
- **Key contribution:** Comprehensive overview of Prosocial Design as an approach to platform governance. Reviews evidence-based design patterns: norm reminders, interstitial friction, content removal effects, thank-you buttons, and recommender system adjustments. Articulates principles: dignity of the user, autonomy, evidence-based design. Distinguishes prosocial design from deceptive patterns.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** The most direct contemporary parallel to Protocol Love's project. Prosocial Design focuses on *patterns* layered onto existing platforms; Protocol Love argues for going deeper — designing prosociality at the constitutive protocol level, not as an add-on.

### 21. Buerger, C., et al. (2025). Blueprint on Prosocial Tech Design Governance. *Toda Peace Institute / Council on Technology and Social Cohesion*.
- **URL:** https://toda.org/assets/files/research/blueprint-on-prosocial-tech-design-governance.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Policy blueprint for prosocial tech design governance. Proposes three tiers: foundational UX, low-barrier designs for prosocial norms, and prosocial algorithms. Argues that harms stem from platform design choices (upstream) rather than content alone (downstream). Recommends mandatory "tech building codes" analogous to architectural codes.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Directly supports Protocol Love's thesis by framing prosocial design as governance. The "tech building codes" concept parallels Protocol Love's constitutive protocols — both argue for prosociality at the foundational design level rather than remedial content moderation.

### 22. Baughan, A., et al. (2025). The Case for Prosocial Tech Design Governance. *Internet Policy Review*.
- **URL:** http://policyreview.info/articles/news/prosocial-tech-design-governance
- **Key contribution:** Policy argument for governing tech design rather than just content. Current regulatory frameworks focus on content removal; prosocial design governance focuses "upstream" on design choices that steer behavior. Compares current content moderation to "purifying a contaminated river one cup at a time."
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** The "upstream" metaphor directly parallels Protocol Love's constitutive approach — designing prosociality at the source (protocol level) rather than remediating downstream effects (content moderation).

### 23. Celadin, T., et al. (2024). Promoting Civil Discourse on Social Media Using Nudges: A Tournament of Seven Interventions. *PNAS Nexus*, 3(10), pgae380. DOI: 10.1093/pnasnexus/pgae380
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11443661/
- **Key contribution:** Pre-registered experiment (N=4,081) testing seven message-based nudges on social media. Finds that nudges making descriptive or injunctive norms salient increase engagement with harmless content; highlighting reputation leads to more substantial comments. No single nudge significantly reduced engagement with harmful content.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows the limits of nudge-based interventions — they can shift engagement patterns but struggle to reduce harmful behavior. Protocol Love argues for constitutive design that goes beyond nudges to make prosociality the structural default.

### 24. Baughan, A., et al. (2024). A Framework for Promoting Online Prosocial Behavior via Digital Interventions. *Nature Human Behaviour*. DOI: 10.1038/s44271-023-00052-7
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s44271-023-00052-7
- **Key contribution:** Presents a framework of existing digital interventions that promote prosocial interactions online, organized into three categories. Notes that most widely used platform interventions (content removal, filtering, banning) are reactive. Calls for more proactive, design-based approaches.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Provides the intervention taxonomy against which Protocol Love's constitutive approach can be positioned — not as another intervention category, but as a fundamentally different level of design.

## Community-Specific Governance Case Studies

### 25. Li, A., et al. (2020). Successful Online Socialization: Lessons from the Wikipedia Socialization Project. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 4(CSCW1), Article 53. DOI: 10.1145/3392850
- **URL:** https://cs.stanford.edu/~diyiy/docs/cscw_li_2020_wiki.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Studies Wikipedia's socialization project for newcomers. 60% of new editors make no edits after their first day. Develops and evaluates socialization interventions based on organizational socialization theory (collective tactics). Shows that structured socialization increases retention and productivity.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates how designed socialization protocols shape community participation. Protocol Love extends this — if socialization protocols can be designed to retain newcomers, constitutive protocols can be designed to produce prosociality as the default mode of engagement.

### 26. Leskovec, J., Huttenlocher, D., & Kleinberg, J. (2010). Governance in Social Media: A Case Study of the Wikipedia Promotion Process. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*.
- **URL:** https://www.cs.cornell.edu/home/kleinber/icwsm10-govern.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Studies Wikipedia's administrator promotion process as a case of deliberative governance. Finds that voting is influenced by relative "figures of merit" (edits, barnstars) and prior interaction. Barnstars (peer recognition awards) significantly predict positive votes.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Wikipedia's barnstar system is an example of protocol-level prosocial design — a recognition mechanism embedded in community structure that constitutively encourages positive contribution. Directly relevant to Protocol Love's case for prosocial protocols.

### 27. Frey, S., Zhong, Q., et al. (2022). Governing Online Goods: Maturity and Formalization in Minecraft, Reddit, and World of Warcraft Communities. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 6(CSCW2). DOI: 10.1145/3555171
- **Key contribution:** Comparative study of governance across three platform types (Minecraft, Reddit, WoW). Analyzes how communities mature and formalize governance over time. Finds common patterns: early informality gives way to codified rules as communities grow.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows governance as an evolutionary process across platform types. Protocol Love argues that if prosocial governance is constitutive (built into protocol architecture), formalization is less necessary because prosociality is the default.

### 28. Hwang, S., & Shaw, A. (2022). Rules and Rule-Making in the Five Largest Wikipedias. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*.
- **Key contribution:** Cross-linguistic study of rule-making across five Wikipedia language editions. Finds different governance cultures and rule structures despite shared technical infrastructure. Demonstrates that governance is culturally situated, not purely technical.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Important counterpoint — even shared protocols produce different governance cultures. Protocol Love must account for cultural variation in how constitutive protocols are enacted and interpreted.

## Moderation Tools and Backchannel Design

### 29. Seering, J., Khadka, M., Haghighi, N., Yang, T., Xi, Z., & Bernstein, M. (2024). Chillbot: Content Moderation in the Backchannel. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 8(CSCW1). DOI: 10.1145/3637362
- **URL:** https://joseph.seering.org/papers/Seering_etal_2024_Chillbot.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Introduces Chillbot, a Discord moderation tool for backchannel communication. Enables moderators to send rapid anonymous feedback in situations where removal is too heavy-handed. Deployed on 11 Discord servers; moderators from 6 continued using it past the study. Shows moderation is not just content removal but relationship management.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that effective moderation involves shaping norms through social interaction, not just enforcement. Protocol Love argues for designing these norm-shaping interactions into the protocol itself.

### 30. Seering, J., & Kairam, S. R. (2023). Who Moderates on Twitch and What Do They Do? Quantifying Practices of Volunteer Moderators. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 7(GROUP), Article 18. DOI: 10.1145/3567567
- **URL:** https://joseph.seering.org/papers/Seering_and_Kairam_2023_Who_Moderates_Twitch_What_Do_They_Do.pdf
- **Key contribution:** Quantitative study of Twitch moderator practices. Finds that positive community-building and socialization is the most common moderator activity across communities of all sizes — more than enforcement. Arguments that moderation tools should "support, rather than supplant, the judgment of users."
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Key finding that moderators primarily do community-building, not enforcement. Protocol Love argues for designing protocols that constitutively perform this community-building function, reducing the need for moderator labor.

### 31. Seering, J., Kraut, R. E., & Dabbish, L. A. (2017). Shaping Pro and Anti-Social Behavior on Twitch Through Moderation and Example-Setting. *Proceedings of the 2017 ACM Conference on Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW)*, 111–125. DOI: 10.1145/2998181.2998277
- **Key contribution:** Studies how moderation actions and example-setting by moderators and community leaders shape prosocial and antisocial behavior on Twitch. Finds that moderator example-setting (positive modeling) is effective at promoting prosocial behavior, complementing punitive moderation.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that prosocial behavior can be shaped through positive modeling, not just punishment. Protocol Love's constitutive approach embeds this modeling into the protocol structure itself (e.g., Journey's cooperative mechanics model prosociality without explicit moderators).

## Federated and Decentralized Governance

### 32. Ermoshina, K., & Musiani, F. (2025). Safer Spaces by Design? Federated Socio-Technical Architectures in Content Moderation. *Internet Policy Review*, 14(1). DOI: 10.14763/2025.1.1827
- **URL:** https://policyreview.info/articles/analysis/safer-spaces-design
- **Key contribution:** Analyzes how federated social media architectures shape content moderation. Argues that federation enables novel practices merging community organizing, information distribution, and alternative techno-social instruments. Asks whether we can speak of "safe spaces by design."
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Directly relevant — federated architectures are an example of constitutive protocol design where the protocol structure itself shapes governance possibilities. Protocol Love can draw on federation as a case where protocol-level decisions constitute the governance environment.

### 33. Hwang, S., Rollins, S., et al. (2025). Decentralizing the Governance of Social Computing Systems. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 9(CSCW).
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.19653
- **Key contribution:** Studies community-driven decentralized governance through co-design workshops. Participants envision an ecosystem of interfaces, tools, and practices built on principles of modularity, forkability, and polycentricity. Proposes that inter-community governance infrastructure could reduce the labor of individual community governance.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Proposes infrastructure-level governance design that distributes and eases governance labor. Protocol Love can draw on modularity and polycentricity as design principles for constitutive protocols that enable prosocial self-governance.

### 34. Lee, J., Doan, B. N., Jee, J., & Seering, J. (2025). Mapping Community Appeals Systems: Lessons for Community-led Moderation in Multi-Level Governance. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 9(CSCW), Article CSCW446. DOI: 10.1145/3757627
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.06557
- **Key contribution:** Studies community appeals systems on Discord as a model for community-led governance within multi-level governance frameworks. Finds that communities design appeals processes that balance scalability, fairness, and accountability while upholding values of growth and rehabilitation.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Appeals systems are a form of constitutive governance design — protocols that embed values of rehabilitation and growth rather than just punishment. Directly supports Protocol Love's argument for prosocial governance at the protocol level.

## Positive Reinforcement and Desirable Behavior

### 35. Goyal, A., Lambert, C., Jain, Y., & Chandrasekharan, E. (2024). Uncovering the Internet's Hidden Values: An Empirical Study of Desirable Behavior Using Highly-Upvoted Content on Reddit. *Proceedings of the International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media (ICWSM)*.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.13036
- **Key contribution:** Uses upvotes as a proxy for community-valued behavior, extracting 64-72 values from 16,000 highly-upvoted comments across 80 subreddits. Finds that existing prosociality metrics capture only ~18% of what communities actually value. Argues for nuanced models of desirability beyond simple prosocial measures.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Demonstrates that community values are richer and more diverse than "prosocial behavior" narrowly defined. Protocol Love should account for this plurality — constitutive protocols need to support diverse expressions of prosociality, not just a single behavioral metric.

### 36. Lambert, C., et al. (2025). Mind Your Ps and Qs: Supporting Positive Reinforcement in Moderation Through a Positive Queue. *arXiv preprint*.
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2509.18437v1
- **Key contribution:** Synthesizes prior work to identify design goals for positive reinforcement in moderation. Implements the "Positive Queue" — a novel moderation system that supplements reactive moderation with mechanisms for highlighting high-quality content and communicating positive community norms.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Positive Queue represents a shift from punitive to appreciative moderation. Protocol Love extends this by arguing that the protocol itself should constitute conditions where positive contribution is the natural, emergent behavior.

### 37. Lambert, C., et al. (2025). What Makes Content Desirable? Leveraging Large Language Models and Causal Inference for Understanding Valued Contributions in Online Communities. *Proceedings of the ACM on Human-Computer Interaction*, 9(CSCW). DOI: 10.1145/3772318.3790476
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.10370
- **Key contribution:** Combines causal inference, predictive modeling, and comparative analysis to identify what makes content desirable. Finds a "policy-practice gap" — most community rules focus on civility and formatting, while the attributes that actually drive positive outcomes (engagement, prosocial anchoring) are absent from written guidelines.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** The policy-practice gap supports Protocol Love's thesis — explicit rules don't capture what actually constitutes prosociality. Constitutive protocol design aims to embed these tacit prosocial mechanisms into the interaction structure itself.

## Wikipedia-Specific Governance

### 38. Kriplean, T., Beschastnikh, I., & McDonald, D. W. (2008). Articulations of WikiWork: Uncovering Valued Work in Wikipedia. *Proceedings of the 2008 ACM Conference on Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW)*, 47–56. DOI: 10.1145/1460563.1460573
- **Key contribution:** Studies Wikipedia barnstars to understand what work is valued in the community. Barnstars constitute a peer recognition system that makes valued contributions visible. Finds that Wikipedia's governance relies on social incentives (recognition) as much as formal rules.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Wikipedia barnstars are a prosocial protocol — a designed recognition mechanism that constitutively encourages positive contribution by making valued work visible and socially rewarded. Directly relevant case study for Protocol Love.

### 39. Halfaker, A., Geiger, R. S., Morgan, J., & Riedl, J. (2013). The Rise and Decline of an Open Collaboration System: How Wikipedia's Reaction to Popularity Is Causing Its Decline. *American Behavioral Scientist*, 57(5), 664–688. DOI: 10.1177/0002764212469365
- **Key contribution:** Studies how Wikipedia's increasingly complex governance (automated tools, reverts, bureaucratic processes) alienated newcomers and contributed to editor decline. Shows that governance mechanisms designed to protect quality can constitutively exclude new participants.

## Ostrom and Commons Governance

### 42. Ostrom, E. (1990). *Governing the Commons: The Evolution of Institutions for Collective Action*. Cambridge University Press.
- **DOI:** 10.1017/CBO9780511807763
- **Key contribution:** Nobel Prize-winning work demonstrating that communities can govern shared resources without privatization or state regulation through self-governing institutions. Identifies eight design principles for successful commons governance: clearly defined boundaries, congruence between rules and local conditions, collective-choice arrangements, monitoring, graduated sanctions, conflict-resolution mechanisms, recognition of self-governance rights, and nested enterprises.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Ostrom's design principles for commons governance are directly applicable to protocol design. Protocol Love's constitutive approach can be understood as embedding Ostrom's principles into the protocol architecture itself — boundaries, collective choice, graduated sanctions, and conflict resolution designed into the interaction structure.

### 43. Frey, S., & Sumner, R. W. (2019). Emergence of Integrated Institutions in a Large Population of Self-Governing Communities. *PLoS ONE*, 14(7), e0216335. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0216335
- **Key contribution:** Studies governance evolution in 5,000+ Minecraft communities. Finds communities develop integrated governance institutions over time, with successful communities adopting multiple complementary governance mechanisms. Links Ostrom's commons governance principles to online community design.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Shows that online communities independently develop Ostrom-like governance. Protocol Love argues that rather than waiting for these institutions to emerge, constitutive protocol design can embed governance principles from the start.

## Behavioral Design and Friction

### 44. Cox, A. L., Gould, S. J. J., Cecchinato, M. E., Iacovides, I., & Renfree, I. (2016). Design Frictions for Mindful Interactions: The Case for Microboundaries. *Proceedings of the 2016 CHI Conference Extended Abstracts on Human Factors in Computing Systems*, 1389–1397. DOI: 10.1145/2851581.2892410
- **Key contribution:** Introduces "design friction" and "microboundaries" — small interaction costs that promote mindful engagement rather than automatic, habitual behavior. Argues that removing all friction is not always desirable; strategic friction can improve user experience and promote reflection.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Design friction is a tool in Protocol Love's constitutive design toolkit. Game case studies (Journey's limited communication, Dark Souls' message system) use strategic friction to shape prosocial interaction — you cannot grief because the protocol constrains interaction to prosocial channels.

### 45. Mildner, T., & Savino, G.-L. (2021). Ethical User Interfaces: Exploring the Effects of Dark Patterns on Facebook. *Proceedings of the 2021 CHI Conference Extended Abstracts on Human Factors in Computing Systems*. DOI: 10.1145/3411763.3451659
- **Key contribution:** Studies "dark patterns" — deceptive design choices that exploit user behavior. Contrasts dark patterns with ethical design. Demonstrates that design choices (choice architecture) can serve prosocial or antisocial ends.
- **Relevance to Protocol Love:** Dark patterns are the inverse of Protocol Love's constitutive prosocial design. If protocols can be designed to exploit (dark patterns), they can equally be designed to care (prosocial protocols). Protocol Love argues for the latter as a design commitment.
