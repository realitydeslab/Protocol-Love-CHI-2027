# Collaborator Discovery — Protocol Love

**Paper:** Protocol Love: Constitutive Protocol Design for Prosocial Worlding  
**Author:** Botao Amber Hu  
**Date:** 2026-04-21

## Potential Collaborators

### 1. Daniel Cook
- **Affiliation:** Independent game designer, Lostgarden (formerly Spry Fox, acquired by Netflix)
- **Key work:** "Kind Games: Designing for Prosocial Multiplayer" (Polaris 2022, GDC 2024); "Prosocial Economics for Game Design" (2019); "Game Design Patterns for Building Friendships" (2016)
- **Collaboration angle:** Cook is the practitioner voice behind the Kind Games movement — the closest existing articulation of Protocol Love's design agenda. Co-authorship or advisory role would bridge theory (Amber) and practice (Cook). Cook's GDC presence gives the work industry visibility. He may provide design pattern validation and case study access.
- **Contact:** Lostgarden blog, GDC network, Twitter/X

### 2. Sarita Schoenebeck
- **Affiliation:** University of Michigan, School of Information (Director, Living Online Lab)
- **Key work:** "Drawing from Justice Theories to Support Targets of Online Harassment" (CHI 2020); "Youth Trust in Social Media Companies" (CSCW 2021); "Online Harassment: Assessing Harms and Remedies" (2023)
- **Collaboration angle:** Leading researcher on restorative justice in platform governance. Protocol Love directly builds on her argument that platforms should embed restorative (care-based) rather than punitive justice. She could strengthen the governance/justice framing and lend credibility in the CSCW community.
- **Contact:** yardi.people.si.umich.edu

### 3. Katherine Isbister
- **Affiliation:** University of California, Santa Cruz (Professor, Computational Media)
- **Key work:** *How Games Move Us: Emotion by Design* (MIT Press 2016); "Enabling Social Play" (2010); extensive work on emotional design in games
- **Collaboration angle:** Leading HCI scholar on emotional and social game design. Her work on how games create empathy through designed interaction is foundational to Protocol Love's affective dimension. Could advise on methodology and strengthen the CHI positioning. Well-connected in both games and HCI communities.
- **Contact:** katherineinterface.com, UCSC

### 4. Joseph Seering
- **Affiliation:** KAIST, School of Computing (South Korea)
- **Key work:** "Reconsidering Community Self-Moderation" (CSCW 2020); extensive work on online community governance, social agents, and moderation
- **Collaboration angle:** His call to move "beyond content removal" toward community-based, proactive moderation aligns directly with Protocol Love's argument for constitutive design. Could contribute the community governance perspective and empirical methodology for studying protocol-level interventions.
- **Contact:** joseph.seering.org

### 5. Emilio Ferrara
- **Affiliation:** University of Southern California (USC), Information Sciences Institute
- **Key work:** "The Gift that Keeps on Giving: Generosity is Contagious in Multiplayer Online Games" (CSCW 2022) — the Sky generosity contagion study
- **Collaboration angle:** His team's empirical study of ~1M Sky players is the strongest quantitative evidence for Protocol Love's thesis. Could contribute large-scale empirical methodology and data access for validating constitutive prosocial design effects. Bridges computational social science with HCI.
- **Contact:** USC ISI

### 6. Jenova Chen
- **Affiliation:** thatgamecompany (Founder/Creative Director)
- **Key work:** Journey (2012), Sky: Children of the Light (2019), Flower (2009); GDC talks on designing emotional social experiences
- **Collaboration angle:** The designer behind Protocol Love's two primary case studies. A foreword, advisory role, or co-authorship would be extraordinarily compelling. Chen's design philosophy ("social means emotional exchange, not number exchange") is essentially the practitioner articulation of constitutive prosocial protocol design. Amber's prior connection to thatgamecompany (listed in industry background) makes this plausible.
- **Contact:** thatgamecompany, GDC network

### 7. Mary Flanagan
- **Affiliation:** Dartmouth College (Sherman Fairchild Distinguished Professor)
- **Key work:** *Values at Play in Digital Games* (MIT Press 2014, with Nissenbaum); Tiltfactor Lab
- **Collaboration angle:** Values at Play is a direct methodological predecessor. Flanagan could help position Protocol Love's constitutive approach against the VAP tradition and strengthen the values-in-design theoretical lineage. Her Tiltfactor Lab also does empirical game studies.
- **Contact:** Dartmouth, Tiltfactor Lab

### 8. Shaowen Bardzell
- **Affiliation:** Georgia Institute of Technology (formerly Indiana University)
- **Key work:** "Feminist HCI: Taking Stock and Outlining an Agenda for Design" (CHI 2010); extensive work on feminist and critical design in HCI
- **Collaboration angle:** Feminist HCI's commitments to ecology, advocacy, and embodiment align with Protocol Love's care-ethics foundation. Bardzell's critical design methodology could strengthen the paper's theoretical positioning. Her work on design qualities maps well onto the protocol design typology.
- **Contact:** Georgia Tech

### 9. J. Nathan Matias
- **Affiliation:** Cornell University (Citizens and Technology Lab)
- **Key work:** "Preventing Harassment and Increasing Group Participation Through Social Norms" (PNAS 2019); large-scale field experiments on community governance
- **Collaboration angle:** Matias does rigorous field experiments on norm-based interventions in online communities. Could help design empirical validation studies for Protocol Love's constitutive design claims. His methods (large-scale Reddit experiments) are a model for testing protocol-level interventions.
- **Contact:** citizensandtech.org, Cornell

### 10. Hideo Kojima
- **Affiliation:** Kojima Productions (Founder/Director)
- **Key work:** Death Stranding (2019), Death Stranding 2 (2025) — "strand game" genre
- **Collaboration angle:** Death Stranding is a key case study — Kojima explicitly designed a game about connection, gifting, and building shared infrastructure. His concept of "strand games" (games about forming connections) is essentially constitutive prosocial protocol design in game form. An advisory or commentary role would add enormous visibility. Less likely than academic collaborators but worth pursuing.
- **Contact:** Kojima Productions
