# Related Works — Protocol Love

**Paper:** Protocol Love: Constitutive Protocol Design for Prosocial Worlding  
**Author:** Botao Amber Hu  
**Venue:** CHI 2027  
**Date:** 2026-04-21  

---

## Positioning Summary

Protocol Love occupies a unique intersection: it bridges **prosocial game design practice** (Kind Games, Journey design talks), **prosocial design theory in HCI** (Values at Play, Value Sensitive Design, Prosocial Design frameworks), **protocol/infrastructure theory** (Galloway, Lessig), **care ethics** (Tronto, Held), and **restorative justice in platform governance** (Schoenebeck). The novel contribution is the **constitutive protocol** framing — arguing that prosociality is not merely an outcome to be nudged or moderated into existence, but can be *constituted* at the protocol level through the rules, affordances, and interaction constraints that define what is possible.

## Key Gaps Protocol Love Fills

1. **Theory-practice bridge:** Kind Games (Cook et al.) is practitioner wisdom without theoretical grounding; Searle/Lessig/Galloway are theory without design guidance. Protocol Love bridges both.

2. **Constitutive vs. regulative distinction in HCI:** Existing prosocial design work (Grüning et al., Prosocial Design Network, nudge theory) tends to treat prosociality as a behavioral outcome to be nudged or incentivized. Protocol Love reframes it as a *structural* property that can be constituted at the protocol level.

3. **From moderation to constitution:** Platform governance literature (Grimmelmann, Gillespie, Seering) focuses on moderation — managing behavior *after* system design. Protocol Love argues for moving governance *into* the design of the system itself.

4. **Care ethics meets protocol theory:** Care ethics (Tronto, Held) and protocol theory (Galloway, Lessig) have not been connected. Protocol Love synthesizes them: protocols as infrastructure for care.

5. **Cross-domain framework:** Existing work is siloed — game design, platform governance, protocol theory, care ethics. Protocol Love provides a unified vocabulary (constitutive protocol design) applicable across domains.
