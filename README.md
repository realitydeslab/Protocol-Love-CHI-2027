# Protocol Love: Constitutive Protocol Design for Prosocial Worlding

**Author:** Botao Amber Hu  
**Venue:** CHI 2027  
**Deadline:** 2026-09-12  
**Status:** Planning  

## Abstract

Digital systems often address antisocial behavior reactively through moderation, sanctions, and policy. This paper argues that prosociality can also be designed constitutively at the level of protocol: through the rules, affordances, currencies, and interaction constraints that define what actions and relations are possible in the first place. We introduce Protocol Love, a conceptual framework for understanding how digital systems cultivate kindness, reciprocity, and care by shaping the structure of participation rather than merely regulating misconduct after it occurs. Through a qualitative comparative case study of prosocial digital worlds—including Journey, Sky: Children of the Light, and Death Stranding—we identify recurring strategies of constitutive prosocial design. These include removing harmful verbs, structuring reciprocal dependence, pacing intimacy through protocol, embedding gift economies, and allowing care to leave durable traces in shared worlds. We contrast these strategies with reactive governance approaches that treat prosocial behavior as an outcome to incentivize or enforce. By foregrounding protocol as a design material for social good, this paper contributes a vocabulary for analyzing prosocial worlds and offers actionable guidance for HCI researchers and designers building systems that do not simply prevent harm, but actively compose the conditions for collective care.
